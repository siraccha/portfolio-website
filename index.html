What I'm All About

In the rush to make survival and advancement possible, we can't forget to make it all worthwhile, too.

By enacting beautiful and human-centered systems that minimize deadweight and encourage healthy habits, we can find more enjoyment in our daily lives and make more time and energy for the things that matter. That's why I do what I do.

I'm all about systems.

I simplify and organize things for better big-picture efficiency, because I know the domino effect of a good system can be huge. Working in the healthcare industry left a lot to be desired in terms of efficiency, and while I did eventually design and implement some solutions during my time in strategic planning, my hunger for better systems has ultimately driven me towards user experience design. In the long term, I'd love to explore different ways of applying the UX design approach to more than just apps and websites, because our non-digital lives are equally as deserving of efficient, human-centered systems as our digital lives are.

Organized, calculated, analytical, but also a wildly creative lunatic.

I consider myself lucky to have done and enjoyed a wide variety of different jobs in my life, but whenever I find myself at the intersection of art and tech, I find myself thinking,

“This is where I belong. Why don’t I settle down here?”

I have yet to find another passion of mine that parallels the rush of designing something (be it an app, a website, a game, a shortfilm, you name it), and then mobilizing (otherwise lifeless) letters, numbers, and tools to give it life and let it act on its own.

“Look at that” says my brain,

“You made that exist. It didn’t exist a minute ago, but it does now. That’s because you thought up the idea and gave it life. Now it’s acting on its own, independent of you, based on instructions you gave it. You’ve made something lifelike, and now this piece of you is free to roam the world for as long as it desires.”

No matter what else I do in my life, I think I’ll keep coming up with ideas and making them exist.
